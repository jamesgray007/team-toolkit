# Content Quality Checklist

11-point quality assurance checklist with detailed explanations and examples for validating all content before publishing.

## How to Use This Checklist

Before publishing any content (email, social post, landing page, course material, sales outreach), verify it meets ALL 11 criteria. Each criterion includes:
- Pass/fail definition
- Good and poor examples
- How to fix if failing

## 1. Business Value First

**Criterion**: Opens with quantified business impact within first 25 words

**Pass Example**:
> "Cut email processing from 90 minutes to 15 minutes daily—save 312 hours annually starting this afternoon."

**Fail Example**:
> "I've been exploring AI tools lately and wanted to share some interesting observations about productivity."

**How to Fix**:
- Start with specific metric (time saved, revenue impact, cost reduction)
- Include timeline (today, this week, within 30 days)
- Front-load the outcome, not the background

**Pass Criteria**:
- [ ] First sentence includes a number (percentage, time, money)
- [ ] Business outcome stated before explanation
- [ ] Under 25 words to the first metric

## 2. Peer Credibility

**Criterion**: Includes reference to similar leaders or recognizable companies

**Pass Example**:
> "Sarah from Salesforce reduced proposal time by 80% using this 3-agent system. Here's the exact framework she implemented."

**Fail Example**:
> "This system can help you create proposals faster using AI automation."

**How to Fix**:
- Name specific person and company (with permission)
- Reference industry or role if can't name ("VP of Product at Fortune 500 SaaS company")
- Include their specific result

**Pass Criteria**:
- [ ] Names a peer, company, or role title
- [ ] Peer is relatable to target audience
- [ ] Includes peer's specific outcome/metric

## 3. Specific Metrics

**Criterion**: Contains at least 2 measurable outcomes (percentages, time savings, dollar amounts)

**Pass Example**:
> "Reduce meeting prep by 75% (from 4 hours to 1 hour weekly) and increase proposal win rate from 23% to 31% within 60 days."

**Fail Example**:
> "Significantly improve your efficiency and see better results over time with our proven approach."

**How to Fix**:
- Replace "significant," "better," "improved" with specific numbers
- Add before/after comparisons
- Include at least 2 different metrics per piece

**Pass Criteria**:
- [ ] At least 2 specific numbers included
- [ ] Numbers show concrete outcomes (not just inputs like "50 videos")
- [ ] Metrics are business-relevant (time, money, conversion rates)

## 4. Implementation Timeline

**Criterion**: Provides clear timeframe for results (today, this week, 30 days)

**Pass Example**:
> "Implement this afternoon in 15 minutes. See first results by tomorrow. Full productivity gains within 7 days."

**Fail Example**:
> "You'll eventually see improvements as you continue using the system over time."

**How to Fix**:
- Add specific dates or durations
- Break into phases if complex (Week 1: X, Week 2: Y)
- Use concrete timeframes: "within 48 hours" not "soon"

**Pass Criteria**:
- [ ] Specific timeframe stated (hours, days, weeks)
- [ ] Timeline is credible and achievable
- [ ] Clear when they'll see results

## 5. Authority Signals

**Criterion**: References proven frameworks, case studies, or implementation experience

**Pass Example**:
> "Based on 200+ enterprise implementations, this framework delivers 3-5x productivity gains. Organizations like Salesforce, Microsoft, and Shopify use similar patterns."

**Fail Example**:
> "This is a great approach that many people find helpful for various situations."

**How to Fix**:
- Add implementation count ("used by X companies")
- Reference frameworks or methodologies
- Cite case studies or outcomes
- Mention teaching/training credentials

**Pass Criteria**:
- [ ] Includes social proof (number of implementations, students, clients)
- [ ] References framework, methodology, or proven approach
- [ ] Demonstrates track record

## 6. Strategic Focus

**Criterion**: Emphasizes business outcomes over technical mechanics

**Pass Example**:
> "Your team will close 40% more deals because AI handles research and initial outreach—freeing reps to focus on relationship-building and negotiations where they add most value."

**Fail Example**:
> "The system uses transformer architecture with attention mechanisms to process natural language and generate contextually relevant responses through token prediction."

**How to Fix**:
- Start with business outcome
- Technical details only if necessary for implementation
- Use analogies instead of architecture explanations
- Focus on "what it does for you" not "how it works under the hood"

**Pass Criteria**:
- [ ] Business outcome stated before (or instead of) technical details
- [ ] Value proposition is clear to non-technical executive
- [ ] No unnecessary jargon or technical architecture

## 7. Actionable Next Steps

**Criterion**: Includes specific, implementable action within 24-48 hours

**Pass Example**:
> "This afternoon: Open ChatGPT and paste this prompt [provided]. Tomorrow: Test on your 3 most common emails. Friday: Train your team on the workflow."

**Fail Example**:
> "Consider exploring these options and think about how they might apply to your specific situation at some point."

**How to Fix**:
- Give exact steps with numbered sequence
- Include specific timeframes for each action
- Provide copy-paste resources (prompts, templates, links)
- Make first step achievable within 24 hours

**Pass Criteria**:
- [ ] Specific action stated (not "think about" or "consider")
- [ ] Clear what to do first
- [ ] Achievable within 24-48 hours
- [ ] Resources provided to enable action

## 8. Competitive Positioning

**Criterion**: Frames as advantage over competitors or market trends

**Pass Example**:
> "While your competitors spend months debating AI strategy, you'll deploy 3 production agents by month-end—gaining a 6-month head start in automation ROI."

**Fail Example**:
> "This tool offers features that could be useful for various business applications."

**How to Fix**:
- Explicitly mention competition ("while others...")
- Frame as early-mover advantage
- Highlight what most aren't doing yet
- Connect to market trends or industry shifts

**Pass Criteria**:
- [ ] References competitive landscape or market position
- [ ] Frames as advantage or differentiation
- [ ] Creates urgency through competitive context

## 9. Executive Language

**Criterion**: Uses business terminology appropriate for VP+ level audience

**Pass Examples**:
- "Strategic implementation" (not "deployment")
- "Orchestrate agents" (not "use tools")
- "ROI, KPIs, competitive advantage" (not "cool features")
- "Technical acumen" (not "technical skills")
- "Accelerate productivity" (not "be more efficient")

**Fail Examples**:
- "Obviously this is really simple"
- "Just sign up and you'll get access to..."
- "This beginner-friendly course teaches basic concepts"
- "You should definitely try this easy approach"

**How to Fix**:
- Replace "should/must/need to" with "based on X, the pattern is..."
- Remove "just," "simply," "easy," "obviously"
- Replace "beginner/basic/intro" with level-appropriate terms
- Use business frameworks over technical tutorials

**Avoid List**:
- "Obviously," "just," "simply," "easy"
- "You should," "you must," "you need to"
- "Beginner," "basic," "intro-level"
- "Theoretical," "academic approach"

**Pass Criteria**:
- [ ] No minimizing language (just, simply, easy)
- [ ] No prescriptive language (should, must, need to)
- [ ] No condescending terms (beginner, basic)
- [ ] Business terminology appropriate for VP+ level

## 10. Clear CTA

**Criterion**: Ends with specific next step and deadline/urgency element

**Pass Example**:
> "Book your 15-minute strategy call by Friday to secure one of 3 remaining January implementation slots."

**Fail Example**:
> "If you're interested, feel free to reach out sometime and we can chat."

**How to Fix**:
- State exact action (book, download, enroll, reply)
- Add specific deadline (by Friday, before Dec 31)
- Include scarcity or urgency (5 spots left, early pricing ends)
- Make it frictionless (one click, 15 minutes, no credit card)

**Pass Criteria**:
- [ ] Specific action verb (book, download, join, reply)
- [ ] Clear deadline or timeframe
- [ ] Urgency or scarcity element
- [ ] Friction reduced (time estimate, ease of action)

## 11. Voice Differentiation

**Criterion**: Content sounds like it could only come from a seasoned builder-educator with 30+ years of experience, not a generic AI content creator

**Pass Example**:
> "Across executive education cohorts, the pattern is consistent: teams that skip the strategy phase spend 3x longer on implementation. The tool isn't the bottleneck — it's the clarity of intent you bring to it. Before adopting any new AI capability, ask your team: 'What decision does this improve, and do we have the judgment to act on better information?'"

**Fail Example**:
> "This amazing new AI tool will 10x your productivity! Here are 5 ways to use it right now. Don't get left behind — the future of work is here and you need to adapt or die."

**How to Fix**:
- Add a human-capability or self-leadership angle (what does this demand of the person, not just what does the tool do?)
- Name a trade-off or nuance (not just benefits — what's genuinely hard about this?)
- Ground insights in pattern recognition from broad experience ("across implementations..." not "I think...")
- Remove hype language, fear-based urgency, and unqualified superlatives
- Ask: "Could someone who started covering AI last year have written this?" If yes, revise.

**Pass Criteria**:
- [ ] Includes a human-capability or self-leadership angle (not just tool features)
- [ ] Names a trade-off or nuance (not just benefits)
- [ ] Reflects pattern recognition from broad experience (not just "I tried this")
- [ ] Could NOT have been written by someone who started covering AI last year

## Scoring and Decision Making

### Scoring System
- **11/11 checks passed**: Publish immediately
- **9-10/11 checks passed**: Minor revisions, then publish
- **7-8/11 checks passed**: Significant revision needed
- **Below 7/11**: Restart from scratch

### Most Critical Checks (Must Pass)
These 6 are non-negotiable:
1. Business Value First
2. Specific Metrics
3. Strategic Focus
4. Actionable Next Steps
5. Clear CTA
6. Voice Differentiation

If any of these 6 fail, do not publish until fixed.

### Priority Order for Fixes
If you're time-constrained and can't hit 10/10:

**Must Fix (8-10)**:
- Business value first
- Specific metrics
- Clear CTA

**Should Fix (6-7)**:
- Strategic focus
- Actionable next steps
- Executive language

**Nice to Fix (4-5)**:
- Peer credibility
- Implementation timeline
- Competitive positioning
- Authority signals

## Quick Self-Check Questions

Before publishing, ask yourself:

1. **Would I open this email?** (Checks 1, 2, 3)
2. **Could I implement this today?** (Checks 4, 7)
3. **Would a VP find this valuable?** (Checks 6, 9)
4. **Is the next step crystal clear?** (Check 10)
5. **Why should they act now?** (Check 8)

If you answer "no" to any, revise before publishing.

## Channel-Specific Variations

### Email
- Checks 1, 3, 7, 10 are critical
- Check 2 (peer credibility) in body
- Check 4 (timeline) in CTA

### LinkedIn
- Checks 1, 2, 8 are critical (hook matters most)
- Check 10 (CTA) can be softer (comment vs buy)
- Check 6 (strategic) essential for executive audience

### Landing Page
- All 10 checks apply to different sections
- Check 1 in hero
- Checks 2, 5 in social proof section
- Check 7 in implementation section
- Check 10 in final CTA

### Sales Outreach
- Checks 1, 2, 8 critical (stand out in inbox)
- Check 3 (metrics) builds credibility
- Check 10 (CTA) should be low-friction first step

## Common Failure Patterns

### Pattern: "Academic Voice"
**Symptoms**: Fails checks 6, 9, 10
**Fix**: Remove jargon, add business outcomes, make CTA direct

### Pattern: "Vague Promises"
**Symptoms**: Fails checks 1, 3, 4
**Fix**: Add specific numbers, timelines, outcomes

### Pattern: "Feature Dump"
**Symptoms**: Fails checks 1, 6, 7
**Fix**: Lead with outcomes, minimize features, add action steps

### Pattern: "Timid Tone"
**Symptoms**: Fails checks 5, 8, 10
**Fix**: Add authority signals, competitive framing, direct CTA

## Final Pre-Publish Checklist

Print this and check before hitting send/publish:

- [ ] 1. Business value in first 25 words
- [ ] 2. Peer or company reference included
- [ ] 3. At least 2 specific metrics
- [ ] 4. Timeline for results stated
- [ ] 5. Authority signal present
- [ ] 6. Business outcome before tech details
- [ ] 7. 24-48 hour action step
- [ ] 8. Competitive positioning
- [ ] 9. Executive-appropriate language
- [ ] 10. Clear CTA with deadline
- [ ] 11. Voice differentiation — seasoned builder-educator voice, not generic AI content

**Score**: _____ / 11
